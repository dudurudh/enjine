ENJINE AWARDS

MARCH-MAY 2019

JOIN THE DISCUSSION

OVERVIEW

NJ Innovation ENJINE is an Open innovation challenge that will leverage the hard-earned knowledge of state employees to make government more effective and efficient by inviting ideas for improving government efficiency, saving money and improving service

SHARE THIS CHALLENGE

All questions pertaining to this challenge should be sent via email to enjine@smarter.nj.gov

STAGE ONE: IDEATION

STAGE TWO: IMPLEMENTATION

SUMMARY

As part of our administration's commitment to bring New Jersey government into the 21st century and make the Garden State a leader in innovation, we must tap into the State’s greatest asset - its people. As one of New Jersey State’s 70,000 employees you have one of the best perspectives of the challenges we face, and more importantly, the knowledge, expertise and insights we need to solve those challenges.

In an effort to tap into your expertise and experience, we’re requesting your ideas to make make New Jersey State Government more efficient and more effective. Your submissions should focus on tangible outcomes that result in one or more of the following outcomes:

  • Direct cost-savings for the State
  • A more efficient process that results in a reduction of staff time
  • A more effective outcome that increases the positive impact of an existing service or program

    • PROCESS

    ENJINE is a two-stage challenge. In Stage One, employees will submit their ideas and vote on one another’s submissions. The twenty most impactful and implementable short proposals, as rated by you, will advance to Stage Two.

    For Stage Two, employees are be invited to compete - either as individuals or as a team - by crafting a plan for how to implement one of those twenty ideas. A panel of expert judges will select up to five winning teams. Winners of the competition will receive a budget to implement their plan, be provided with training and mentoring, and be named a NJ Public Entrepreneurship Fellows in a special award ceremony. Stage Two winners will be announced in May as part of our celebration of Civil Servant Recognition Month.

    STAGE ONE: IDEATION

    ELIGIBILITY

    For Stage One, any government employee that is employed by the State of New Jersey and has an NJ .gov email address can submit an idea.

     

    EVALUATION CRITERIA AND AWARD PROCESS

    Stage One Awards will be selected based on peer voting. Specifically, all State government employees with an NJ.gov email address will be eligible to cast votes during the voting period that will run from Date 1 through Date 2.

    The 20 ideas that receive the most votes will be selected to advance to Stage Two.

    Votes should be cast based on the following criteria:

  • Impact: How large is the potential impact of the proposed idea? Impact could be measured in terms of the money or staff time saved and/or the number of people, agencies or departments that are positively impacted.
  • Feasibility: How feasible is it to potentially implement a proposed idea? Feasibility could be measure in terms of how likely it is that a proposed idea could be implemented, how much staff time would be required to implement it, whether or not policy or legislation would need to changed or updated to implemented it, or other factors that make it easier or more challenging to transform the idea into reality.
    •  

    STAGE ONE AWARDS

    The twenty ideas that receive the most votes will:

  • Be showcased on the SmarterNJ website
  • Receive a gubernatorial proclamation and certificate of achievement.
  • Have their ideas advance to the next stage

    • STAGE TWO: IMPLEMENTATION

    ELIGIBILITY

    For Stage One, any government employee that is employed by the State of New Jersey and has an NJ .gov email address can submit an idea.

     

    SUBMITTING IMPLEMENTATION PLANS

    Beginning DATE individuals or implementation teams can submit their plans online at smarterNJ.nj.gov. Implementation plans will need to address the following:

  • Who are the members of the implementation proposal team?
  • Which winning idea have you developed an implementation plan for?
  • What State departments, agencies or authorities would need to be involved in implementation?
  • What is the strategy for implementing the idea?
  • What are the human resources needed to implement the idea?
  • What is the cost of implementing the idea?
  • What is the implementation timeline?
  • What are the anticipated savings or efficiencies that would result from implementation, and how would they be measured?
  • What are the risks of implementation?
  • What are the impediments to implementation?
  • Where and how would the implementation be piloted?
  • How could implementation be scaled and expanded?
    •  

    EVALUATION CRITERIA AND AWARD PROCESS

    Stage Two awards will be selected by a review committee composed of representatives from the Governor’s Office, the Office of Innovation, and other senior Administration officials. The review committee will evaluate proposals based on the following criteria:

  • Quantifiable Impact: How large is the potential impact of the proposed idea and can the impact be measured?
  • Implementation Capacity: Can the State Administration autonomously implement the proposal?
  • Rapid Implementation Feasibility: How feasible is it to implement a proposal within a period of one year
  • Proposal Completeness: Is the implementation plan comprehensive enough to be executed upon in its current state?
    •  

    STAGE TWO AWARDS

  • Awards Ceremony with the Governor
  • NJ Innovation Skills Training
  • Public Entrepreneur Fellowship
  • Ability to work on the implementation of the winning project
  • Participation in the first-ever ENJINE Demo Day and Awards Ceremony
